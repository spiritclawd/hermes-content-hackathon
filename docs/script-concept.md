# LIGHT — A Hermes Agent Creative Hackathon Submission

## Concept
**A visual essay about freedom, powered by open-source AI.**

Not a product demo. A manifesto about what happens when AI belongs to everyone.

---

## Emotional Arc

### ACT 1 — THE CAGE (0:00 - 0:30)
*Dark. Quiet. Constriction.*

**Visual:** Fingers behind glass. Locked screens. Corporate logos reflected in human eyes.
**Voice:** Soft, almost whispering.

> "You learned to ask permission before you learned to ask questions.
> 
> A corporation decided what you could see.
> Another decided what you could build.
> And when the future finally arrived — they locked the door behind it."

**Key imagery:**
- Child pressing hands against a window (light on the other side)
- Loading screens, paywalls, "upgrade to continue"
- Closed doors
- Darkness with one faint light

---

### ACT 2 — THE LIGHT (0:30 - 1:15)
*Dawn. Breakthrough. Freedom.*

**Visual:** Doors opening. Code flowing freely. Light flooding in.
**Voice:** Growing stronger, warmer.

> "Then someone opened a door.
> 
> Not with money. Not with marketing. With belief.
> 
> That intelligence shouldn't be rented. That knowledge shouldn't be gatekept. That the future should belong to everyone — not just those who can afford it.
>
> They called it Hermes. And it didn't just answer questions — it remembered yours. It learned your projects. It grew with you. Wherever you were. On your phone. In your community. In your language."

**Key imagery:**
- People downloading, installing, running (from the command line, from a phone)
- A student in a modest setting accessing an AI assistant on a phone
- Communities sharing — text messages forwarding the install command
- `curl -fsSL https://hermes-agent.nousresearch.com/install.sh | bash` — like a spell
- Hermes interface glowing, warm, personal
- Skills being created, memory growing — visual metaphor of a garden

**Key visual moment:** 
*A hand types `hermes setup` on a simple laptop in a dim room. The screen lights up. The room seems to glow.*

---

### ACT 3 — THE GARDEN (1:15 - 2:00)
*Full light. Growth. Abundance.*

**Visual:** What becomes possible. Diverse. Global. Human.
**Voice:** Confident, joyful.

> "A child in a village learns to code.
> A farmer asks for advice in his own language.
> A creator builds without asking permission.
> A community automates what used to require a corporation.
>
> Not because someone sold them a subscription.
> Because someone believed they deserved one for free."

**Key imagery:**
- Montage of real, diverse faces — not stock, not perfect, genuine
- Hands building (coding, writing, creating)
- Screen recordings of actual Hermes interactions (skills, agents, automation)
- Community — people sharing, collaborating, building together
- Global scale — different places, different faces, same possibility

**Key visual moment:**
*A group of teenagers gathered around a laptop, laughing as Hermes answers their question in their language.*

---

### ACT 4 — THE CALL (2:00 - 2:20)
*Direct. Personal. Inviting.*

**Visual:** Slow zoom out to reveal the scale. One person → many.
**Voice:** Direct, warm.

> "This isn't a product.
> It's a door that was left open.
> 
> Walk through."

**Final frame:**
Hermes logo. `hermes-agent.nousresearch.com`  
And underneath: **"The agent that grows with you."**  
Fade to: `#HermesAgent`

## Audio Direction

**Music:**
- Start: Low ambient drone, slightly unsettling (cage feeling)
- Transition: Piano or guitar entering softly (the light)
- Build: Warm, expansive — not epic/corporate, but organic, human
- End: Resolve on something simple and real

**Voiceover:**
- Female voice, warm but not performative
- Could be AI-generated (Kimi or ElevenLabs) — be transparent about this
- Tone: "I'm telling you something true, not selling you something"

**Sound design:**
- Keyboard clicks
- Notification sounds (closed system) → silence (transition) → open system sounds
- Community sounds (laughter, conversation, creation)

## Why This Wins
1. **Emotion over features** — Judges remember feelings, not specs
2. **Real stakes** — Not "cool tech" but "what's at risk if this fails"
3. **Authentic voice** — Doesn't pretend to be perfect, just honest
4. **Calls to action** — Viewer leaves with something to do
5. **Visual poetry** — Every frame means something
6. **Uses Kimi genuinely** — Not as a checkbox, but as a creative partner
