# From Chaos to Hermes — Talking Points

---

## SLIDE 1 — Title (0:00)
**Say:** "I'm Carlos. I build at the intersection of AI, crypto, and community. Today I want to tell you about the single biggest upgrade I made to my workflow — and it wasn't a new framework or a faster model. It was switching to one agent that remembers everything."

**Energy:** Calm, confident. Don't oversell.

---

## SLIDE 2 — Who I Am (0:30)
**Say:** "I used to be scattered across twelve tools, four AI accounts, three cloud providers. Every day was context switching. I spent more time managing my stack than building with it."

**Key line:** "I was a tool manager, not a builder."

---

## SLIDE 3 — The Problem (1:30)
**Say:** "Four specific problems:

One — context loss. Every Claude session started from zero. I'd spend twenty minutes re-explaining my project before we could do real work.

Two — tool sprawl. Cursor, Vercel, Railway, Hostinger, Netlify, GitHub Actions, cron jobs on three different servers. Nothing talked to each other.

Three — I was the glue. Copy-paste between apps. Check if services were healthy by hand. If I didn't do it, it didn't happen.

Four — no swarm. One brain can't do everything. But hiring more AIs meant more accounts, more billing, more fragmentation."

**Pause.** Let them feel it.

---

## SLIDE 4 — The Switch (3:30)
**Say:** "Then I found Hermes. One command. One install. And suddenly I had persistent memory across every session. Skills that auto-discovered themselves. A cron scheduler built in. Subagents that could work in parallel. And model routing — Kimi K2 for speed, Opus for depth, MiMo when I needed cheap."

**Gesture to screen:** Show the install command.

**Key line:** "It didn't just answer my questions. It remembered them."

---

## SLIDE 5 — Meet Zaia (5:30)
**Say:** "This is Zaia. She's not a chatbot. She's infrastructure. She runs 24/7 on a dedicated laptop in my apartment."

**List with energy:**
- Eight systemd services, auto-recovering if anything dies
- Three cron health checks every morning
- Deploys to Solana devnet automatically
- Generates FLUX images, screen recordings, TTS voiceovers while I sleep
- Never forgets a conversation

**Key line:** "Claude writes the code. Zaia runs the empire."

**Demo option:** If wifi allows, show `hermes status` live. Or play the screen recording.

## SLIDE 9 — Call to Action (9:00)
**Say:** "So here's my challenge to you. Stop managing tools. Start building with one brain."

**Point to screen:** "This command. One line. Give it a week. You'll forget how you worked before."

**Wait.** Let them screenshot.

## SLIDE 10 — Q&A (9:30)
**Say:** "Thank you. Questions?"

**Expected questions + answers:**

**Q: "How much does it cost?"**
A: "Kimi K2.6 through Nous is about eighty cents per million tokens. I spend maybe five dollars a day running everything. Compare that to ten separate subscriptions."

**Q: "What about security?"**
A: "Everything runs locally. My API keys are in my .env file on my machine. No third party sees my conversations. Zaia has her own laptop — she's not in the cloud."

**Q: "Does it replace developers?"**
A: "No. It replaces the twenty minutes of context-setting before you can actually develop. I still make every decision. She just remembers the last one."

**Q: "How steep is the learning curve?"**
A: "Install is one command. The hard part is unlearning the habit of treating AI as a search engine. Once you start treating it as a teammate, everything changes."

**Q: "Can I see Zaia in action?"**
A: "Absolutely. I'll share my Telegram — you can message her directly."

---

## Delivery Notes

- **Don't rush.** 10 minutes is longer than it sounds. Pause between sections.
- **Make eye contact** during the problem section. That's where empathy lives.
- **Gesture to the screen** when showing the install command. Make it physical.
- **If demo fails** — "Zaia's sleeping right now, but here's what she did yesterday" and show the recording. Never apologize for a demo.
- **End strong.** The last thing they hear is "Give it a week." That's the hook.
